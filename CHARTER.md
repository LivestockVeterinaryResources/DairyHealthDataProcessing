# Project Charter

> **Status: DRAFT for community review.** This charter is proposed by the
> founding contributors and is intended to be discussed, amended, and ratified
> by the community. Items marked `<TODO>` need confirmation before adoption.

## Mission

To improve **cattle health and welfare** through better, data-driven decisions —
built as **open, reproducible, community-owned tooling** that any veterinarian,
researcher, producer, or student can use, inspect, trust, and improve.

## Scope

This project covers tooling, methods, documentation, and teaching materials for
working with **cattle health data** broadly: importing and standardizing records,
creating analysis-ready data, computing health and production metrics, and
producing reports. The scope is intentionally broad so the community can grow
into new areas of cattle health and welfare over time. It is **not** narrowed to
any single disease, product category, vendor, or data source.

## Core values

These values are the standard against which every contribution — and every offer
of support — is judged. They are our safeguard for credibility.

1. **Scientific integrity & methodological transparency.** Every analytical
   choice is visible in the code, documented, and open to review. We do not hide
   how a number is produced.
2. **Reproducibility.** Anyone should be able to re-run the tooling and verify
   the results.
3. **Vendor and data-source neutrality.** The code serves the science and the
   animals — not any drug maker, genetics company, equipment vendor, software
   platform, or data provider. No commercial interest receives favorable
   treatment in how data is handled, computed, or interpreted.
4. **Welfare and decision quality first.** When interests conflict, animal health
   and welfare and the quality of the resulting decisions take precedence.
5. **Open by default.** Open license, open development, open discussion. The
   community's right to fork is permanent and is never given away.

## Who we are

A public, collaborative community of veterinarians, researchers, producers,
data analysts, educators, and students interested in improving animal health and
welfare. Contribution takes many forms, all valued:

- **Expertise** — domain review, methodology, study design, documentation,
  teaching, issue triage.
- **Code** — features, fixes, tests, examples, infrastructure.
- **Data** — example datasets and test fixtures contributed for open use.
- **Resources** — organizations contributing staff time, funding, or
  infrastructure **to support** the above.

Industry and commercial organizations are explicitly **welcome as participants
and resource providers** — on the same terms as everyone else, defined in
[GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) and [SPONSORSHIP.md](SPONSORSHIP.md).

## The participation principle

> **Influence is earned through contribution and judged on merit and scientific
> soundness — never purchased.**

Funding and resources buy *capacity* (more time, more people, more
infrastructure). They do not buy governance seats, merge authority, or any
control over methodology, analysis, or interpretation. This principle is what
lets us welcome industry support without compromising independence.

## Stewardship intent

The founding contributors act as **stewards, not owners**, of this project. The
project's code, name, and any future assets are intended to be held for the
benefit of the community, with a path toward a **neutral, independent home**
(e.g., a fiscal host or nonprofit) as the project matures. See
[GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md).
